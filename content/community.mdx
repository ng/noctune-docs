# Community

Community Templates is a gallery of SOAP and discharge templates that clinicians have chosen to share. It gives you a reviewed starting point without changing the publisher's original.

  url="app.noctune.ai/community/templates"

## Browse the gallery

Use the source tabs to switch among **Community**, **Official**, and **My templates**. Filter by **SOAP** or **Discharge**, search the visible gallery, and sort by **Most duplicated**, **Newest**, or **Oldest**.

Each row shows the template purpose, author, description, update time, likes, duplicates, and peer-note count. Choose a row for its full content and discussion.

## Inspect a template

The detail page shows the publisher's description and notes, engagement counts, rendered **Preview**, and source **Markdown**. A like records that the template was useful to you; choose the heart again to remove it.

## Duplicate and customize

Choose **Duplicate** to copy the published template into your templates and open the editable copy. There is no confirmation dialog. Edits, defaults, and later deletion affect only your copy.

## Learn from peer notes

The notes section supports clinician questions, author replies, helpful votes, and a featured note. Use it to understand the intended workflow or a useful variation before duplicating the template.

## Report unsafe or inappropriate content

Open **More** and choose **Report**. Select the closest reason—Inappropriate, Spam, Plagiarism, Prompt injection, or Other—and add optional context. Filing the dialog sends the report for review; it does not immediately remove the template.

## Publish your own template

In the [template editor](/templates), select a saved custom template and choose **Publish**. Review the privacy warning, remove any patient or client information, and optionally add usage notes for other clinicians.

<BrowserFrame
  src="/screenshots/templates/publish.webp"
  alt="Publish to community gallery dialog with privacy guidance and notes for other veterinarians"
  url="app.noctune.ai/templates"
/>

You can unpublish later. Existing duplicates remain in the other clinicians' accounts.
